Statement

I have been attracted by physics since I was a child, and so lucky was I to be admitted by USTC, which has teh best School of Physics in China. Those excellent works done by Prof.Jianwei Pan and his colleagues leaded me into the field of quantum information science and multi-photon entanglement. I joined the group supervised by Prof.Chaoyang Lu, who is an oustanding young scientist around the world. There I learned a lot about basic theory and optical experiment techniques.

After I finidhed my B.Sc. thesis advised by Prof.Luming Duan in Tsinghua, I join Tsinghua as a Ph.D. student. I've been interested in graphene since the amazing Nobel Prizes won by A.Geim, K.Novoselov and Scoth tape. The combination the this amazing material and quantum computation made my excited, so I devode myself into fabrication of graphene quantum dots.

We did meet with much trouble in this project, but even worse is that our lab has always been in lack of collaboration with others. We have known that to fabricate the BN-Graphene-BN device, one needs to get high quality BN sample from some Japanese lab. Scientists all over the world choose to collaborate with them, with no exception. However, we chose to buy bulk BN from a company. After two months' trial, we found the BN sample is doped more highly than we expected, and there's always a leakage current. Another instance is that we did have some results, but our PI didn't want to collaborate with those groups of computational materials. As a new estabilshed lab, we didn't have much members, or in detail we just had an associate research fellow, a posdoc, me, and two pregraduate students then. So the calculation was delayed until today.

Actually, I feel somewhat depressed about this situation. In the famous paper "How to Pick a Graduate Advisor", the author Ben A.Barres said, "If the day arrives when you are in graduate school when you wake up and do not wish to jump out of bed and ahead off to lab, it is time ti consider whether it is time to switch to another lab." And I make sure it is the time for me to join a top-class lab.

I've been looking forward to living in Europe for years. Europe is the cradle of the Renaissance and Industrial Revolution, and I hope to breathe the air and feel the atmosphere there. By the way, it's definitely better than Beijing, where you could only get fresh air in the clean room.
